Queen Elizabeth II

Former Queen of the United Kingdom and other Commonwealth realms

Here's a time line of Queen Elizabeth II's life:

  • 1926 - Born 21 April in Mayfair, London, England, United Kingdom
  • 1942 - On her 16th birthday, Elizabeth carries out her first public engagement when she inspects the Grenadier Guards, of which she had been appointed colonel-in-chief.
  • 1947 - Queen Elizabeth II married to Lt Philip Mountbatten at Westminster Abbey. He is thenceforth known as the Duke of Edinburgh.
  • 1952 - King George VI dies and Queen Elizabeth II succeeds to the throne. She is in Kenya when her father dies â€“ the first British monarch since George I to be out of the country at the time of succession.
  • 1953 - Coronation of Queen Elizabeth II at Westminster Abbey in the first televised coronation service. The Queen and the Duke of Edinburgh embark on a tour of the Commonwealth.
  • 1965 - The Queen visits West Germany, the first British monarch to do so since the first world war.
  • 1977 - Queen Elizabeth II marked the Silver Jubilee of her accession
  • 2002 - Queen Elizabeth II marked her Golden Jubilee, the 50th anniversary of her accession.
  • 2012 - Elizabeth's 2012 Diamond Jubilee marked 60 years on the throne, and celebrations were held throughout her realms, the wider Commonwealth, and beyond. She and her husband undertook an extensive tour of the United Kingdom, while her children and grandchildren embarked on royal tours of other Commonwealth states on her behalf.
  • 2022 - Elizabeth's Platinum Jubilee began on 6 February 2022, marking 70 years since she acceded to the throne on her father's death. On the eve of the date, she held a reception at Sandringham House for pensioners, local Women's Institute members and charity volunteers.[226] In her Accession Day message, Elizabeth renewed her commitment to a lifetime of public service, which she had originally made in 1947.
  • 2022 - Died 8th September (aged 96) Balmoral Castle, Aberdeenshire, Scotland, United Kingdom
